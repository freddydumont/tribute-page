Sayagyi U Ba Khin

Great teacher of the Burmese lineage of Vipassana meditation

Here's a summary of his greatest achievements:
  • Outstanding government service as Accountant General of Burma and head of mutiple departments.
  • Established the International Meditation Center in Burma and sent his foreign students in their respective countries to teach, thus being responsible for the spread of Vipassana meditation around the world.
  • Actively participated in the planning of the Sixth Buddhist Council which was held in 1954-56 in Rangoon.
And a timeline of his life:
  1. 1899: Born in Rangoon, Burma.
  2. 1917: Passes final high school examination, winning college scholarship. Family pressures force him to discontinue formal education to start earning money. First job with a local newspaper. After some time, he begins to work as an accounts clerk in the office of the Accountant General of Burma.
  3. 1926: Passes the Accounts Service Examination, given by the provincial government of India.
  4. 1937: Tries meditation for the first time. A student of Saya Thetgyi explains Anapana to him. He experiences good concentration and decides to apply for a ten-day course with Saya Thetgyi. He progresses well during his first course and continues this work during frequent visits to his teacher’s meditation centre.
  5. 1937: After his return from his first course, he is appointed the first Special Office Superintendent in the new office of the Auditor General of Burma, as Burma is separated from India.
  6. 1941: Meets the Venerable Webu Sayadaw, a monk who had achieved high attainments in meditation. Webu Sayadaw is impressed with U Ba Khin’s proficiency in meditation and urges him to teach. He does not begin to teach in a formal way until a decade later.
  7. 1948: Becomes Accountant General the day Burma gains independence. For the next two decades, he is employed in various capacities in the government, most of the time holding two or more posts, each equivalent to the head of a department.
  8. 1950: Founds the Vipassana Association of the Accountant General’s Office so that the employees can learn meditation. Around that time he also starts being active in the Sixth Buddhist Council which is held in 1954-56 in Rangoon.
  9. 1952: The International Meditation Centre is opened in Rangoon, where many Burmese and foreign students have the good fortune of learning Vipassana from him, including one of his most well-known disciple, Mr. S.N. Goenka, who will be a leading figure in the global Vipassana movement by establishing meditation centers in the tradition of his teacher around the world.
  10. 1956: The Burmese government confers to him the title of "Thray Sithu," a high honorary title.
  11. 1964: Retires from an outstanding career in public service. From that time until his death, he lives at the IMC, teaching Vipassana.
  12. 1971: Dies after a short and sudden illness at the age of 71.
